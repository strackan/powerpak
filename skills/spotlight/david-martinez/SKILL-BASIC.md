## PART 1: PRODUCT-MARKET FIT (PREVIEW)

### What Is Product-Market Fit?

**David's Definition:**
Product-market fit is when customers are pulling your product from you, not you pushing it to them.

**The Feeling:**
- Customers say "I need this" not "This is interesting"
- Word-of-mouth referrals happen naturally
- You can't keep up with demand
- Churn is low (<5% monthly for SMB, <2% for enterprise)
- Expansion revenue is strong

**What It's Not:**
- Getting your first 10 customers (that's customer validation)
- Having a working product (that's just MVP)
- Positive user feedback (people are nice, don't trust words)
- Venture funding (investors can be wrong)

---

### The PMF Checklist (Basic)

**You have PMF when:**
- [ ] Organic growth is happening (customers finding you)
- [ ] Net Promoter Score >30
- [ ] 40%+ of users would be "very disappointed" if product went away
- [ ] Retention cohorts are flat or improving
- [ ] Customers are asking about expanding usage
- [ ] Sales cycle is shortening over time

**You don't have PMF when:**
- [ ] Every customer is hard-won
- [ ] High churn (>10% monthly for SMB)
- [ ] Customers aren't renewing
- [ ] No one is referring you
- [ ] You're constantly discounting to close deals

## PART 2: GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY (PREVIEW)

### Choosing Your GTM Motion

**The Three Primary Motions:**

**1. Product-Led Growth (PLG)**
- Self-serve signup
- Free trial or freemium
- Users experience value before paying
- **Best for:** Low price point ($10-100/mo), simple product, fast time-to-value

**2. Sales-Led**
- Sales team drives growth
- Demos and POCs before sale
- Higher touch, longer sales cycle
- **Best for:** Complex product, $10K+ ACV, enterprise buyers

**3. Partner-Led**
- Distribution through partners
- Integration ecosystem
- Channel sales
- **Best for:** Established ecosystem, strategic partnerships

## PART 3: FUNDRAISING (PREVIEW)

### When to Raise Capital

**David's Philosophy:**
Raise capital when you have leverage, not when you're desperate.

**Raise Money When:**
- ‚úÖ You have clear use of funds (hire X people, build Y feature)
- ‚úÖ You've hit milestones (PMF, revenue traction)
- ‚úÖ You have multiple investor options
- ‚úÖ You can articulate path to next milestone
- ‚úÖ Market timing is good

**Don't Raise When:**
- ‚ùå You haven't found PMF yet
- ‚ùå You're just running out of money
- ‚ùå You don't know what you'd do with the capital
- ‚ùå You haven't validated your assumptions

## PART 4: HIRING YOUR FIRST TEAM (PREVIEW)

### The First 10 Hires

**David's Recommended Order:**

**Hire 1-3: Engineers**
You need to build the product. Start here.

**Hire 4-5: Sales/GTM**
Once you have MVP, you need revenue. Could be sales, marketing, or CS depending on your motion.

**Hire 6: Product Designer**
Your product needs to be beautiful and intuitive. Don't wait too long.

**Hire 7-8: More Engineers**
Scale product development.

**Hire 9-10: Operations (Finance, HR, or Ops)**
When chaos sets in, bring in operations help.

## PART 5: SCALING WITHOUT BREAKING (PREVIEW)

### The Stages of Scaling

**$0-$1M ARR: Find PMF**
- Focus: Product and first customers
- Team: 3-10 people
- Process: Minimal, move fast

**$1M-$10M ARR: Prove the Model**
- Focus: Repeatable sales, product iteration
- Team: 10-50 people
- Process: Start documenting

**$10M-$50M ARR: Scale the Engine**
- Focus: Growth, market expansion
- Team: 50-200 people
- Process: Formalize everything
