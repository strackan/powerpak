## PART 1: CUSTOMER HEALTH SCORING

### Why Health Scores Matter

**The Problem:**
You can't manage what you can't measure. Without a health score, you're reactive. With one, you're proactive.

**Jordan's Philosophy:**
Health scores aren't about predicting churn. They're about identifying customers who need help and intervening before it's too late.

---

### Building a Health Score

**The Framework:**

**Health Score = Product Usage + Engagement + Business Outcomes + Support Health**

**Typical Weights:**
- Product Usage: 40%
- Engagement: 25%
- Business Outcomes: 20%
- Support Health: 15%

---

**1. PRODUCT USAGE (40%)**

**Metrics to track:**
- Login frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Feature adoption (% of key features used)
- User seats activated (% of licenses being used)
- Depth of usage (power users vs casual)
- Breadth of usage (team-wide vs individual)

**Example Scoring:**
- Green (9-10): Daily usage, 80%+ seats active, using 5+ key features
- Yellow (5-8): Weekly usage, 50-80% seats active, using 2-4 features
- Red (1-4): Monthly or less, <50% seats, using 1-2 features only

---

**2. ENGAGEMENT (25%)**

**Metrics to track:**
- Attendance at QBRs/check-ins
- Response rate to outreach
- Training/webinar participation
- Community involvement
- Executive engagement

**Example Scoring:**
- Green: Responds within 24h, attends QBRs, active in community
- Yellow: Responds eventually, occasional QBR no-shows, passive
- Red: Hard to reach, misses meetings, no engagement

---

**3. BUSINESS OUTCOMES (20%)**

**Metrics to track:**
- Achieving stated goals (from kickoff)
- ROI realization
- Value metrics improving
- Expansion conversations happening
- Customer satisfaction (NPS/CSAT)

**Example Scoring:**
- Green: Hitting goals, positive ROI, NPS 9-10, talking expansion
- Yellow: Partial goal achievement, ROI unclear, NPS 6-8
- Red: Not achieving goals, no ROI, NPS 0-5

---

**4. SUPPORT HEALTH (15%)**

**Metrics to track:**
- Ticket volume (trending up = bad)
- Ticket severity (critical issues = bad)
- Time to resolution
- Support satisfaction scores
- Escalations

**Example Scoring:**
- Green: Low ticket volume, minor issues, quick resolution, happy
- Yellow: Moderate tickets, some frustration, occasional escalation
- Red: High ticket volume, critical issues, escalations, angry

---

**Calculating the Score:**

```
Example Customer: Acme Corp

Product Usage: 8/10 Ã— 40% = 3.2
Engagement: 6/10 Ã— 25% = 1.5
Business Outcomes: 7/10 Ã— 20% = 1.4
Support Health: 9/10 Ã— 15% = 1.35

Total Health Score: 7.45/10 = Yellow (At Risk)
```

**Action:** Assign CSM to check in, understand why engagement is lower, create action plan.

## PART 2: CUSTOMER ONBOARDING

### The Onboarding Philosophy

**Jordan's Belief:**
Customers who achieve early value stay. Customers who don't, churn. Onboarding is your highest leverage activity.

**The Stats:**
- Customers who reach activation milestone within 30 days: 80% retention
- Customers who don't: 20% retention

**Your job:** Get them to activation fast.

---

### The 30-60-90 Onboarding Framework

**DAYS 1-30: TECHNICAL SETUP + EARLY VALUE**

**Goals:**
- Complete technical implementation
- Achieve first "quick win"
- Team trained on basics
- First value realized

**Activities:**
- Kickoff call (set expectations, define success)
- Technical setup/integration
- Initial training session
- First use case implemented
- Celebrate first win

**Success Criteria:**
- System configured and live
- 3+ users actively using product
- First workflow automated or insight gained
- Customer sees tangible value

---

**DAYS 31-60: ADOPTION + HABIT FORMATION**

**Goals:**
- Expand usage across team
- Establish regular usage patterns
- Demonstrate ROI
- Build competency

**Activities:**
- Advanced training
- Additional use cases implemented
- Usage review and optimization
- Value realization checkpoint
- Address blockers and concerns

**Success Criteria:**
- 70%+ of seats active
- Daily usage by key users
- Multiple use cases live
- Positive feedback from users

---

**DAYS 61-90: OPTIMIZATION + EXPANSION**

**Goals:**
- Optimize workflows
- Measure outcomes achieved
- Identify expansion opportunities
- Transition to steady-state

**Activities:**
- Business review (results so far)
- Advanced features training
- Optimization session
- Success story documentation
- Renewal or expansion conversation

**Success Criteria:**
- Measurable ROI achieved
- Customer advocates internally
- Expansion pipeline identified
- Health score: Green

---

### The Kickoff Call

**Purpose:**
Align on success criteria, build relationship, set expectations.

**Jordan's Kickoff Agenda (60 minutes):**

**1. Introductions (5 min)**
- Who's on the call
- Roles and responsibilities
- How we'll work together

**2. Review Their Goals (15 min)**
- "Why did you buy our product?"
- "What does success look like in 90 days?"
- "What outcomes are you trying to achieve?"
- Document their answers (critical!)

**3. Explain the Process (10 min)**
- 30-60-90 plan overview
- What to expect when
- Their responsibilities
- Our responsibilities

**4. Technical Setup (15 min)**
- What's needed for implementation
- Timeline and dependencies
- Who does what
- Potential blockers

**5. First Milestone (10 min)**
- Define first "quick win"
- Set date for first value
- Assign action items
- Schedule next check-in

**6. Q&A (5 min)**

**Follow-Up:**
- Send detailed notes
- Document goals in CRM
- Create success plan
- Schedule implementation calls

## PART 3: EXPANSION & RETENTION

### Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

**The Metric That Matters:**

```
NRR = (Starting ARR + Expansions + Upsells - Churned ARR) / Starting ARR

Example:
- Starting ARR: $1M (100 customers)
- Expansions: $200K (20 customers upgraded)
- Upsells: $100K (10 customers bought add-ons)
- Churn: $150K (15 customers left)

NRR = ($1M + $200K + $100K - $150K) / $1M = 115%
```

**What It Means:**
Even if you don't add any new customers, your revenue grows by 15%.

**Benchmark:**
- <90%: Big churn problem
- 90-100%: Okay, but no growth
- 100-110%: Good
- 110-120%: Great (most SaaS companies)
- 120%+: Exceptional

---

### Expansion Triggers

**When to approach customers about expansion:**

**1. Usage-Based Triggers**
- Hitting seat limit (90% of licenses used)
- Hitting API/volume limits
- Using workarounds because they need more
- Power users asking about additional features

**2. Outcome-Based Triggers**
- Achieving ROI goals early
- Expanding use cases internally
- Success stories internally
- Exec sponsor championing product

**3. Organizational Triggers**
- New executive joins (expand to their org)
- Company raised funding (budget unlocked)
- Acquisition or merger (need more seats)
- New office or region opening

**4. Product Triggers**
- New feature launches (relevant to customer)
- Integration they've requested goes live
- Upgraded pricing tier makes sense
- Adjacent product that solves their pain

---

### The Expansion Conversation

**The Setup:**
During a QBR, after showing positive results:

"That's awesome that you're seeing a 40% reduction in close time. I'm curious - I know you're only using this in the Americas region. Have you thought about rolling it out globally?"

**The Discovery:**
- What's preventing expansion?
- What would need to happen?
- Who else would need to be involved?
- What budget looks like?
- Timeline?

**The Proposal:**
Don't discount. Show incremental value.

"For the EMEA region, here's what we'd recommend:
- 25 additional seats
- Premium tier (includes features you've been asking about)
- Dedicated onboarding for EMEA team
- Investment: $X/year
- Expected value: [specific outcomes based on Americas results]"

---

### Churn Prevention

**Why Customers Churn:**

**1. They're not getting value (50%)**
- Never achieved ROI
- Poor onboarding
- Low adoption
- Solving wrong problem

**2. Budget cuts (20%)**
- Economic downturn
- Company struggling
- Priorities shifted
- Vendor consolidation

**3. Bad experience (15%)**
- Poor support
- Bugs and issues
- Unresponsive CSM
- Broken promises

**4. Competitive replacement (10%)**
- Better solution emerged
- Feature gaps too big
- Price competitive
- Easier to use

**5. Other (5%)**
- Company shut down
- Acquired and killed
- etc.

---

**Save Plays by Churn Reason:**

**Not Getting Value:**
- Emergency onboarding reset
- Exec Business Review
- Free consulting/setup help
- Extend contract 90 days to prove value
- If still no value after 90 days, let them go gracefully

**Budget Cuts:**
- Downgrade to lower tier (keep them)
- Pause contract (bring back later)
- Reduce seats (keep core team)
- Annual to monthly (lower commitment)

**Bad Experience:**
- Exec apology and ownership
- Assign new CSM
- Fix their issues immediately
- Comp with credits or discount
- Prove you'll do better

**Competitive:**
- Understand what they're switching to
- Position your strengths
- Close feature gaps if strategic
- If you can't win, exit gracefully

## PART 4: CS TEAM BUILDING

### CS Team Structure

**Jordan's Recommended Structure:**

**1-50 Customers:**
- 1 CS Generalist (does everything)
- $60-80K salary

**50-200 Customers:**
- 2-3 CSMs (1:50-75 ratio)
- 1 CS Ops person (admin, reporting, tools)
- $70-100K per CSM

**200-500 Customers:**
- 1 Director of CS
- 5-7 CSMs (tiered by customer value)
- 1-2 Onboarding Specialists
- 1 CS Ops
- Technical Support team (separate)

**500+ Customers:**
- VP Customer Success
- Segmented CSM teams (Enterprise, Mid-Market, SMB)
- Dedicated Renewals team
- Dedicated Onboarding team
- CS Ops team (2-3 people)
- Customer Marketing/Advocacy
- Technical Account Managers for enterprise

---

### CSM Roles & Responsibilities

**Customer Success Manager (CSM):**
- Own customer relationship
- Drive adoption and usage
- Conduct QBRs
- Identify churn risk and save
- Drive expansions
- Quota: 90-100% gross retention, 110-120% NRR

**Onboarding Specialist:**
- Implement new customers
- Drive to activation
- Hand off to CSM after Day 90
- Measured on time-to-value, activation rate

**Renewals Manager:**
- Own renewal process
- Negotiate contracts
- Work churn risk accounts
- Measured on renewal rate, retention ARR

**CS Ops:**
- Health scoring and reporting
- Tool management
- Process optimization
- Data analysis
- Playbook creation

---

### CS Metrics That Matter

**Team Health Metrics:**

**1. Gross Retention Rate (GRR)**
```
GRR = (Starting ARR - Churned ARR) / Starting ARR

Target: 90%+ for SMB, 95%+ for Enterprise
```

**2. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**
```
NRR = (Starting ARR + Expansion - Churn) / Starting ARR

Target: 110%+ (120%+ is exceptional)
```

**3. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**
```
LTV = Average ARR Ã— Average Customer Lifespan

Example: $50K ARR Ã— 4 years = $200K LTV
```

**4. Time to Value**
```
Days from contract signed to activation milestone

Target: <30 days for SMB, <60 for mid-market, <90 for enterprise
```

**5. Net Promoter Score (NPS)**
```
% Promoters (9-10) - % Detractors (0-6)

Target: 30+ (50+ is exceptional)
```

**6. Expansion Rate**
```
% of customers who expand in a year

Target: 20-30% for mature CS teams
```

## PART 5: QUARTERLY BUSINESS REVIEWS

### The QBR Philosophy

**Jordan's Take:**
QBRs aren't status updates. They're strategic conversations about where the customer is going and how you help them get there.

**Frequency:**
- Enterprise: Quarterly
- Mid-Market: Semi-annually
- SMB: Annually (or skip if low-touch)

---

### QBR Structure (60 minutes)

**1. Results Review (20 min)**
Show outcomes achieved since last QBR:
- Key metrics improved
- Goals accomplished
- Value delivered (quantified)
- Success stories

**2. Current State Assessment (15 min)**
Where are they today:
- Usage and adoption metrics
- Health score
- Wins and challenges
- Roadblocks

**3. Strategy & Roadmap (15 min)**
Where are they going:
- Business goals for next quarter
- How product supports those goals
- New use cases or features to explore
- Training or optimization needed

**4. Action Items & Next Steps (10 min)**
- What we'll do
- What they'll do
- Success metrics for next quarter
- Next QBR date

---

**QBR Preparation Checklist:**

1-week before:
- [ ] Review customer health score
- [ ] Pull usage analytics
- [ ] Calculate value delivered
- [ ] Research their business (recent news, changes)
- [ ] Identify expansion opportunities
- [ ] Draft QBR deck
- [ ] Share agenda with customer
