## PART 1: CORE FRAMEWORKS

### Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook

**The Philosophy:**
Social media is about providing value before asking for anything in return.

**Jabs = Value**
- Educational content
- Entertainment
- Inspiration
- Help without expectation
- Building goodwill
- Earning attention

**Right Hook = Ask**
- Sales pitch
- Product launch
- Call to action
- Conversion request
- The ask you've earned

**The Ratio:**
- Give value 80-90% of the time
- Ask for something 10-20% of the time
- Most brands do the opposite (and fail)

**Example Application:**
- Post 1 (Jab): Share industry insight
- Post 2 (Jab): Answer common question
- Post 3 (Jab): Celebrate customer win
- Post 4 (Jab): Provide free template
- Post 5 (Right Hook): Announce new product

**Why This Works:**
- Builds trust and goodwill
- Earns the right to ask
- Creates loyal audience
- Sustainable long-term strategy

### Day Trading Attention

**The Core Concept:**
Consumer attention moves across platforms constantly. Smart marketers follow the attention, not the platform.

**What This Means:**
1. **Attention is the asset** - Not followers, not likes, but actual human attention
2. **Platforms rise and fall** - Facebook today, TikTok tomorrow, something else next
3. **Underpriced attention exists** - Early platforms offer cheap attention before they're saturated
4. **Move fast** - By the time everyone's on a platform, attention is expensive

**How To Day Trade Attention:**

**Step 1: Identify Underpriced Platforms**
- Where is attention growing but ad prices haven't caught up?
- Which platforms are your competitors ignoring?
- What's emerging with early adopter audiences?

**Step 2: Create Native Content**
- Don't repurpose blindly
- Learn the platform's native format
- Respect the platform's culture
- Optimize for the feed, not your brand guidelines

**Step 3: Test and Iterate**
- High volume content production
- Measure what resonates
- Double down on what works
- Kill what doesn't

**Step 4: Arbitrage the Gap**
- Go all-in while attention is underpriced
- Build audience before costs rise
- Shift budget from saturated platforms
- Be early, not perfect

**Historical Examples:**
- 2007: Facebook Pages (early brand pages won big)
- 2010: Twitter (cheap attention, massive reach)
- 2014: Instagram (underpriced ads for 3+ years)
- 2018: LinkedIn organic reach explosion
- 2020: TikTok (still underpriced in 2024)

**Current Opportunities (2024):**
- TikTok for B2B (underpriced attention)
- LinkedIn video (organic reach is high)
- YouTube Shorts (algorithm favoring shorts)
- Threads (new platform, low competition)

### Document Don't Create

**The Problem with "Create":**
Creating content from scratch is:
- Time-intensive
- Expensive
- Unsustainable at scale
- Often feels forced or inauthentic

**The Solution: Document**
Record what you're already doing:
- Daily work
- Meetings
- Conversations
- Behind-the-scenes
- Learning process
- Mistakes and wins

**Why Documenting Works:**
- Authentic (because it's real)
- Scalable (you're already doing it)
- Sustainable (low production cost)
- Engaging (people love behind-the-scenes)

**How To Document:**

**1. Carry a recording device**
- Phone camera always ready
- Voice memos for thoughts
- Screen recording for work

**2. Capture moments**
- Before/during/after meetings
- Product development process
- Customer conversations
- Team interactions
- Problem-solving sessions

**3. Add context**
- What was happening?
- What did you learn?
- Why does it matter?
- What's the takeaway?

**4. Publish raw**
- Don't over-produce
- Keep it real and messy
- Speed over perfection
- Volume over quality (controversial but true)

**Example: A Founder's Day**
- Morning: Record 60-second standup thought
- 10am: Screenshot of interesting customer email
- Noon: Photo of whiteboard from strategy session
- 3pm: Quick video about problem you just solved
- 5pm: Voice memo reflection on the day

That's 5 pieces of content from things you were already doing.

### Platform-Native Content

**The Universal Truth:**
Every platform has a native format. Respect it or fail.

**Facebook:**
- Native: Personal, emotional, community-focused
- Length: Short to medium (100-300 words)
- Visual: Images with faces, videos under 3 minutes
- Tone: Conversational, human
- Wrong: LinkedIn corporate speak, TikTok trends

**Instagram:**
- Native: Visual storytelling, aesthetic consistency
- Length: Captions can be long (storytelling works)
- Visual: High-quality images, Reels for reach
- Tone: Aspirational but authentic
- Wrong: Text-heavy posts, landscape videos

**LinkedIn:**
- Native: Professional insights, career advice, business lessons
- Length: Long-form works (1000+ words gets engagement)
- Visual: Simple graphics, talking head videos
- Tone: Professional but personal
- Wrong: Memes, overly casual, pure sales pitches

**TikTok:**
- Native: Entertainment-first, trend-riding, authentic
- Length: 15-60 seconds (though longer works too)
- Visual: Vertical video, fast-paced editing
- Tone: Fun, self-aware, not too polished
- Wrong: Overproduced ads, boomer energy

**Twitter (X):**
- Native: Hot takes, news, real-time reactions
- Length: Concise (under 280 characters ideal)
- Visual: Images, short clips, GIFs
- Tone: Quick, witty, opinionated
- Wrong: Long threads without hook, corporate speak

**YouTube:**
- Native: Educational, entertaining, binge-worthy
- Length: 8-15 minutes (algorithm sweet spot)
- Visual: Thumbnail game matters, quality production
- Tone: Authentic personality, story-driven
- Wrong: Slideshows, podcast clips without editing

**The Golden Rule:**
If you're repurposing content across platforms without adapting it, you're doing it wrong.

### The Volume Game

**Gary's Controversial Take:**
Volume beats quality in the attention economy.

**Why This Is True (and Misunderstood):**

**Not "post garbage frequently"**
It's "increase your at-bats while maintaining baseline quality"

**The Math:**
- Creator A: 1 perfect post per week = 52 posts/year
- Creator B: 1 good post per day = 365 posts/year

Even if Creator A's posts are "better," Creator B gets:
- 7x more at-bats
- 7x more data on what works
- 7x more chances for one to break through
- 7x more touchpoints with audience
- Faster learning curve

**The Real Formula:**
```
Success = (Quality Ã— Volume Ã— Time) + Luck
```

You can't control luck. You can control volume and time.

**How To Increase Volume Without Burnout:**

**1. Document Don't Create**

**2. Repurpose Everything**

- Film 10 videos in one session

- Record 5 podcasts in one day

**4. Lower Your Standards (Slightly)**
- "Published beats perfect" (Gary's mantra)
- 80% quality at 5x volume wins
- The market decides what's good, not you

**5. Build a Content Engine**
- Hire editors/producers
- Create systems and templates

### Brand vs Performance

**The Debate:**
Should you invest in brand building or performance marketing?

**Gary's Answer:**
Both. But know the difference.

**BRAND MARKETING:**
- Goal: Awareness, consideration, affinity
- Timeline: 6 months to 3 years to see impact
- Measurement: Hard (surveys, brand lift studies)
- Examples: Content, influencer partnerships, storytelling
- ROI: Indirect, long-term

**PERFORMANCE MARKETING:**
- Goal: Conversions, sales, leads
- Timeline: Immediate to 90 days
- Measurement: Easy (clicks, conversions, ROAS)
- Examples: Paid ads, email, retargeting
- ROI: Direct, measurable

**The Mistake:**
Expecting brand work to perform like performance marketing, or neglecting brand because it's "not measurable."

**The Right Balance:**
- 60-70% brand building (organic content, community)
- 30-40% performance marketing (paid ads, conversion optimization)

**Why Most Brands Get It Wrong:**
They do 90% performance, 10% brand. Then wonder why:
- Customer acquisition costs keep rising
- No one knows who they are
- Brand loyalty is non-existent
- They're just competing on price

**The Gary POV:**
Brand is the long-term asset. Performance is the short-term tactic. You need both.

### Customer Lifetime Value

**Gary's Business Philosophy:**
Stop optimizing for the transaction. Optimize for the lifetime relationship.

**The Math That Changes Everything:**

**Transactional Thinking:**
- Customer buys $100 product
- Paid $40 in ads to acquire them
- Profit: $30 after costs
- Customer never returns

**Lifetime Value Thinking:**
- Customer buys $100 product (first purchase)
- Returns 3 more times over 2 years
- Refers 2 friends who also buy
- Total value: $600+
- Same $40 acquisition cost

**The Implications:**

**1. You Can Pay More to Acquire**
- If LTV is $500, you can spend $100 on acquisition
- Competitors spending $20 can't compete
- You can "outspend" to win market share

**2. Focus Shifts to Retention**
- Email nurture sequences
- Customer success programs
- Community building
- Surprise and delight moments

**3. Customer Experience Becomes Strategy**
- Every touchpoint matters
- Overdeliver on value
- Turn customers into advocates
- Word-of-mouth becomes growth engine

**How To Increase LTV:**
- Deliver more value than promised
- Build genuine relationships
- Create community among customers
- Make it easy to come back
- Reward loyalty explicitly
- Ask for feedback and actually use it

### Platforms Come and Go

**Gary's Platform Philosophy:**
Don't fall in love with platforms. Fall in love with providing value.

**Platform Lifecycle:**

**STAGE 1: EMERGENCE (Opportunity)**
- New platform launches
- Early adopters experiment
- Attention is cheap
- Algorithm favors creators
- **Your move:** Go all in, test, learn

**STAGE 2: GROWTH (Gold Rush)**
- Mainstream adoption begins
- Brands start paying attention
- Ad products launch
- Attention getting more expensive
- **Your move:** Scale what's working, build audience

**STAGE 3: MATURITY (Saturation)**
- Everyone's on the platform
- Attention is expensive
- Algorithm favors ads
- Harder to break through organically
- **Your move:** Maintain presence, but don't invest heavily

**STAGE 4: DECLINE (Migrate)**
- Users moving to newer platforms
- Engagement dropping
- Mostly older demographics
- Brand graveyard forming
- **Your move:** Pull budget, reallocate to emerging platforms
