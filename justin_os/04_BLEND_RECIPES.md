## TOP 5 RECIPES

### 1. THE AUTHENTIC FOUNDER
**Components:** O1 (Vulnerability) + M1 (Story Arc) + E2 (Invitation)
**When to use:** Product launches, founder updates, building trust
**Typical performance:** High comments (50-100), moderate shares (10-20)
**Energy match:** Melancholy, reflective, honest
**Example topics:** Layoff stories, product journeys, personal transitions
**Why it works:** Vulnerability creates connection, story provides structure, invitation enables action

### 2. THE PATTERN SPOTTER
**Components:** O4 (Pattern Recognition) + M2 (Philosophical) + E5 (Application)
**When to use:** Industry commentary, thought leadership, trend analysis
**Typical performance:** High shares (20-40), moderate comments (30-50)
**Energy match:** Philosophical, analytical, insightful
**Example topics:** Market observations, category insights, future predictions
**Why it works:** Pattern = "I see what you did there", philosophy = depth, application = utility

### 3. THE STORYTELLER
**Components:** O3 (Scene-Setting) + M6 (Dialogue) + E6 (Philosophical Button)
**When to use:** Customer stories, real interactions, lived experience
**Typical performance:** Moderate shares (15-25), high comments (60-90)
**Energy match:** Present, observational, reflective
**Example topics:** Customer conversations, conference moments, real-world encounters
**Why it works:** Vivid scene draws in, dialogue feels real, philosophy elevates

### 4. THE PROVOCATEUR
**Components:** O5 (Provocative Question) + M2 (Philosophical) + E5 (Redirect)
**When to use:** Hot takes, challenging assumptions, stirring debate
**Typical performance:** High shares (30-50), very high comments (100+, often contentious)
**Energy match:** Punchy, aggressive, contrarian
**Example topics:** Industry criticism, unpopular opinions, "everyone's wrong about X"
**Why it works:** Provocation stops scroll, philosophy backs it up, redirect offers alternative

### 5. THE CONNECTOR
**Components:** O6 (Specific Detail) + M4 (Analogy) + E1 (Open Question)
**When to use:** Community building, relationship development, connection-seeking
**Typical performance:** Very high comments (80-120), moderate shares (12-18)
**Energy match:** Warm, inviting, curious
**Example topics:** Customer wins, shared experiences, "does this resonate?"
**Why it works:** Specificity = credible, analogy = relatable, question = invitation

## EXPERIMENTAL BLENDS (Testing)

### THE RABBIT HOLE POST
**Components:** Any opening + F7 (Absurdist Rabbit Hole) + Brief callback ending
**Status:** Signature move - works consistently
**When to use:** When the tangent IS the post
**Energy match:** Playful, contrarian, intellectually curious
**Typical performance:** Very high engagement (people share the wtf story)
**Structure:** 
- Open with main point (50-100 words)
- Gateway word triggers tangent (300-500 words)
- Brief return to main point (50 words)
- The tangent is the star
**Why it works:** Unexpected depth creates "wait what?" moment, dark/ironic edge makes it memorable, callback grounds it

### THE ABSURDIST CONNECTOR
**Components:** O2 (Absurdist) + M4 (Analogy) + E3 (Callback)
**Status:** Testing
**Hypothesis:** Absurd opening + grounding analogy + satisfying structure = memorable
**Next test:** Event invitation

### THE TECHNICAL STORYTELLER
**Components:** O3 (Scene-Setting) + M3 (Technical Deep Dive) + E5 (Application)
**Status:** Testing
**Hypothesis:** Story draws in non-technical, deep dive proves expertise, application lands for both
**Next test:** Product explainer

### THE VULNERABLE PROVOCATEUR
**Components:** O1 (Vulnerability) + M7 (Evidence + Vulnerability) + E1 (Open Question)
**Status:** Testing
**Hypothesis:** Leading with vulnerability softens provocation, makes hot take human

## FAILED BLENDS (Don't Repeat)

### THE CORPORATE PHILOSOPHER
**Components:** O5 (Provocative) + M2 (Philosophical) + E6 (Philosophical Button)
**Why it failed:** Too heavy, no relief, felt preachy
**Lesson:** Need lightness somewhere in structure

### THE LIST POST
**Components:** O4 (Pattern) + M5 (List) + E5 (Application)
**Why it failed:** Too LinkedIn-generic, lost Justin voice
**Lesson:** Lists need heavy commentary to work, or skip them

## BLEND PERFORMANCE BY GOAL

**Want high engagement (comments)?**
→ Use: Connector, Storyteller, Authentic Founder

**Want high reach (shares)?**
→ Use: Provocateur, Pattern Spotter

**Want both?**
→ Use: Provocateur (but be ready for spicy comments)

**Want relationship building?**
→ Use: Connector, Authentic Founder

**Want thought leadership positioning?**
→ Use: Pattern Spotter, Storyteller
