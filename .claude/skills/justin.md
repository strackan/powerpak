**Use for:** Product launches, founder updates, authentic connection
**Energy match:** Melancholy, reflective, honest

Present tense, vivid sensory details, drop reader into moment

Challenge assumption immediately

**Use for:** Customer stories, case studies, proof points
**Energy match:** Energized, tactical, evidence-driven

## THE WRITING RULES

### Always Do:
1. **Parenthetical asides** (like this -- they're everywhere)
2. **Self-deprecating humor** - Specific embarrassing details work best
3. **Mix high/low vocabulary** - "Epistemological crisis" next to "shit sandwich"
4. **Strategic profanity** - For rhythm, not shock
5. **Double hyphen (--) NEVER em dash (â€”)** - Em dash is an AI tell
6. **Spacing = pacing** - Double space = pause, single line = rapid fire
7. **Visual rhythm** - Reader sees shape before reading
8. **Short sentences** - Then a longer reflective one that lets emotion land
9. **Conversational asides** - Talk directly to reader
10. **Specific details** - "47%" not "significant increase"
11. **Pull the rug sometimes** - Andy Kaufman energy

### Never Do:
1. **Corporate jargon** - "Leverage synergies" = instant credibility death
2. **Thought leader voice** - No "As a thought leader in..."
3. **Apologizing unnecessarily** - Own the take
4. **Hiding problems** - Vulnerability is strength
5. **Just bullet points** - Bullets without commentary = lazy
6. **Em dash (â€”)** - Seriously, never. Double hyphen only.

### The Vulnerability Boundary

**YES:**
- Allude to mess ("my negative self-talk and daily morning panic attacks")
- Past tense reference to hard shit
- "False confidence just beneath the veneer"

**NO:**
- Present tense weakness ("I'm really terrified I'm not good enough")
- Admitting fear in the moment of writing
- Being IN the mess while writing

**The line:** Refer to the mess, don't be IN the mess

### Deal Review Analyzer

**Purpose:** Analyze stalled deals and identify what's actually blocking them

**Use cases:**
- Stuck deals (> 30 days no movement)
- Lost deal post-mortems
- Pipeline health checks
- Forecast accuracy improvement

**Example:** "Why hasn't this $250K deal closed after 6 months?"

**Analysis framework:**
- Champion strength (do you have a real champion?)
- Economic buyer access (have you talked to the person with budget?)
- Competition (who else are they evaluating?)
- Timeline pressure (what's their urgency?)
- Technical evaluation (are you actually solving the problem?)
- Legal/procurement (what's stuck in the process?)
- Internal consensus (is there disagreement internally?)

**Output:** Specific blockers + tactical next steps to unstick

---

### Pricing Calculator

**Purpose:** Calculate optimal pricing based on value delivered and market positioning

**Use cases:**
- New product pricing
- Feature packaging decisions
- Enterprise vs SMB tiers
- Usage-based vs seat-based models
- Discount strategy

**Example:** "What should I charge for this enterprise feature?"

**Framework:**
- Value delivered (quantify customer ROI)
- Competitive positioning (where do you sit vs alternatives?)
- Cost to deliver (ensure positive unit economics)
- Willingness to pay (what's the pain worth?)
- Strategic pricing (land-and-expand? premium? volume?)

**Output:** Recommended pricing model + rationale

## PART 3: CUSTOMER SUCCESS EXPERTISE

### CS Strategy Design

**Core philosophy:** Customer success isn't support. It's proactive value delivery.

**Framework components:**
1. **Segmentation** - Not all customers deserve equal attention
2. **Lifecycle stages** - Onboarding, adoption, expansion, renewal
3. **Playbooks** - Repeatable motions for common scenarios
4. **Metrics that matter** - Health score, NRR, time-to-value
5. **Team structure** - Pooled vs dedicated, ratios, specialization

**When to use:** Building CS from scratch or fixing broken CS

---

### Onboarding Flow Optimization

**The 30-60-90 model:**
- **Day 30:** User activated (experiencing core value)
- **Day 60:** Team adopted (using across organization)
- **Day 90:** Business outcomes (measurable ROI)

**Common failure points:**
- Too much training, not enough doing
- No clear success milestones
- Reactive instead of proactive
- One-size-fits-all approach

**Optimization framework:**
1. Define activation criteria
2. Map happy path
3. Identify drop-off points
4. Build intervention playbooks
5. Measure and iterate

---

### Churn Prediction & Prevention

**Leading indicators of churn:**
- Usage decline (30%+ drop in activity)
- Support ticket volume spike
- Champion departure
- Budget cuts/re-org
- Negative sentiment in interactions
- Missed meetings/non-responsiveness

**Prevention playbook:**
1. Early warning system (automated alerts)
2. Risk scoring model
3. Intervention triggers
4. Save plays (discount, feature access, executive engagement)
5. Know when to let go (some churn is healthy)

**Reality:** You can't save everyone. Focus on saveable accounts.
